Who am I?

I am a PhD student at Goldsmiths, University of London (but based in York), as part of the IGGI Doctoral Training Center (find out more here). I am currently active in AI and Machine Learning applications for Games research with a focus on the ways in which we can use Deep Learning to model various facets of video games streams (e.g. stream highlights, emotional moments, in-game events, various streamer behaviours etc.) with a focus on how to fuse multi-view stream data (e.g. audio, web-cam footage, game footage, chat) into a single model.

What do I do?

  • I solve problems using Machine Learning, especially focusing on Deep Learning and multi-view data. I do this using Keras and Python.
  • I explore and experiment with other approaches to Artificial Intelligence e.g. Video Game AI and Evolutionary Algorithms.
  • I design and make games, normally developing in Unity. I am espically interested games with a focus on novel AI and/or interactions with AI.
  • I enjoy giving talks and (informally) teaching people about Machine Learning and AI.

Interested?

If you are interested in me or my work I would love to hear from you. I am interested in freelance work or consultancy as well as discussing ideas and collaborating on projects. Using one of the links below to get in touch.
